Chapter 2 Problems

Parsing

Parsing problems in Chapter 2 are similar to those in Chapter 1, but require you to parse conjunctions, disjunctions, and biconditionals as well.

Due to the informal convention that a set of parentheses may be dropped on either side of a biconditional and in the antecedent or consequent of a conditional, steps 3 and 4 should always be performed before step 5.

To complete parsing problems:

  1. Perform steps 1-4 of the instructions from Chapter 1 Parsing.
  2. Negations are treated as in Chapter 1. If a negation sign occurs in the sentence outside of any pair of parentheses but is not a well-formed negation, then it is either informally-correct sentence of some other type, or it is not well-formed.
  3. If the string of symbols has a biconditional double arrow not contained by any parentheses, or contained only by one set of parentheses, click on it. If the string is a well-formed biconditional, then the arrow will blink green and each side of the biconditional will appear below it.
  4. Conditionals are treated as in Chapter 1.
  5. If the string of symbols has a conjunction or disjunction sign not contained by any parentheses, or contained only by one set of parentheses, click on it. If the string is a well-formed is a well formed conjunction or disjunction, then the sign will blink green and each conjunct or disjunct will appear below it.
  6. Repeat steps 2, 3, 4, and 5 for the resulting sentences.
  7. If all remaining bottom-most sentences are atomic, you are finished parsing the sentence. If the sentence is a negation, click 'Official Notation'. If the sentence is not a negation and is contained by parentheses, click 'Official Notation'. Otherwise, click 'Unofficial Notation'.
  8. If none of steps 2, 3, 4, or 5 could be carried out, click 'Not Well Formed'.
  9. Click 'Check' or use keyboard shortcut to confirm that your work is correct and complete.
