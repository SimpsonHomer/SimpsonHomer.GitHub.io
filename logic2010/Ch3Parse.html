Chapter 3 Problems

Parsing

Parsing problems in Chapter 3 are similar to those in Chapter 2, but require you to parse universal and existential generalizations, as well.

To complete parsing problems:

  1. Perform steps 1-4 of the instructions from Chapter 1 Parsing.
  2. Negations are treated as in Chapter 2. If a negation sign occurs in the sentence outside of any pair of parentheses, and outside the scope of any quantifier, but is not a well-formed negation, then it is either informally-correct sentence of some other type, or it is not well-formed.
  3. Biconditionals are treated as in Chapter 2.
  4. Conditionals are treated as in Chapter 1.
  5. If the sentence consists of a formula that occurs entirely within the scope of a universal or existential quantifier, click on the quantifier. If the sentence is a well-formed generalization, the quantifier will blink green and the formula, aside from the quantifier, will appear below the sentence.
  6. Conjunctions and disjunctions are treated as in Chapter 2.
  7. If the sentence consists of a predicate and either a constant or a variable, click on the predicate. It will blink green and the constant or variable will appear below the formula.
  8. Repeat steps 2 through 7 for the resulting sentences.
  9. If all remaining bottom-most sentences are sentence letters, constants, or variables, you are finished parsing the sentence. If the sentence is a negation or generalization, click 'Official Notation'. If the sentence is neither and is contained by parentheses, click 'Official Notation'. Otherwise, click 'Unofficial Notation'.
  10. If none of steps 2 through 7 could be carried out, click 'Not Well Formed'.
  11. Click 'Check' or use keyboard shortcut to confirm that your work is correct and complete.
